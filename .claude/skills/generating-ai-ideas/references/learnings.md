# Learnings for generating-ai-ideas

This sidecar captures the distilled, permanent lessons from two major phases of real work on the skill:

- 20 systematic council composition experiments (removing CMO, testing sizes from 3 to 7+, introducing User Reality, Differentiation & Moat Strategist, and Distribution & Channel Realist).
- 9 subsequent live real-world invocations of the skill on diverse AI opportunity seeds.

These lessons are the authoritative source for how the council should be structured and operated.

## Core Structural Lessons

**Smaller, high-agency teams outperform large balanced councils.**
- 4–5 person "Reality Teams" or "Kill Teams" consistently produced higher idea quality, better flaw detection, stronger conviction, and better signal-to-noise than 6–7 person versions.
- The original 7-person "business school" council (Moonshot + Antagonizer + Researcher + CFO + CMO + COO + Legal) showed measurable bloat and lower conviction in both the 20 experiments and the 9 live runs.
- CMO was the weakest contributor across the entire body of work and has been permanently removed.

**Antagonizer + Researcher is the non-negotiable foundation.**
- Almost every top-scoring composition started with this pair.
- They are the highest-leverage combination for cutting through AI hype with real evidence.

**Two official modes (codified after the 20 experiments and validated in the 9 live runs):**

1. **Fast Mode (Core 3)**: Moonshot + Antagonizer + Researcher
   - Best for: Broad requests, early triage, high-volume screening.
   - In live use: Dramatically improved efficiency (see Live Run 4 and Live Run 8). Always log the mode used.

2. **Standard Strong Mode** (recommended default for serious work)
   - Core: Antagonizer + Researcher
   - Add selectively using the Role Selection Decision Guide in reference.md.
   - Typical size: 4–5 people.

**Highest-value roles (ranked by impact across 29 total runs):**
- **User Reality Specialist** — The single most transformative addition. Especially powerful on emotional, identity, creator livelihood, education, and human-consequence products. Frequently turned good outputs into excellent ones when paired with Differentiation.
- **Differentiation & Moat Strategist** — Excellent partner to Antagonizer. Raises the bar on structural ownability rather than traditional positioning.
- **Distribution & Channel Realist** — Critical for marketplace, platform, and acquisition-heavy ideas.
- **COO + Legal** — Remain very high value in operational, hardware, or high-liability contexts (the "Kill Team 4" configuration is still excellent for agent/SaaS with maintenance burden).

**Moonshot usage note**: Useful for initial expansion on some seeds, but often better applied selectively (early or as a late "make it bigger" pass) rather than as a permanent default member in Standard Strong Mode.

## Vertical-Specific Patterns

**Creator, educator, and cohort-based tools**:
- User Reality + Differentiation & Moat Strategist is the highest-performing 4-person combination.
- Identity ("my students learn *my* way") and emotional labor (reviewing dozens of recordings, managing cohort energy) are frequently the real sources of differentiation and WTP.
- Public Reddit signals are often weaker than private Discords, paid communities, and founder interviews.

**Professional services / high-stakes knowledge work** (lawyers, accountants, consultants, executives):
- These verticals require higher Researcher effort because public "I would pay" language is more guarded.
- Trust, liability anxiety, and reputation risk dominate decision-making.
- Onboarding friction tolerance is extremely low — solos will abandon anything that feels like "another system I have to manage."
- Add extra emphasis in User Reality, Legal, and COO on these dynamics.

**Hardware + software hybrids and privacy-sensitive tools**:
- User Reality + Legal + COO combinations perform well.
- Past hype backlash (Sense, Nest, Humane, Rabbit, etc.) is powerful negative evidence that strengthens Differentiation arguments.

**Marketplace and platform ideas**:
- Antagonizer + Researcher + Differentiation + Distribution is a strong "market reality" team.
- Liquidity death spirals, builder fatigue, and "we don't need another" signals are extremely common and must be taken seriously.

## Operational Lessons for Running the Skill

- Always start broad requests in Fast Mode unless the seed is already narrow and high-conviction.
- Log the exact mode + roles used in every Council Test Notes block (this is now mandatory).
- When Researcher signals feel thin in creator or professional verticals, prioritize closed communities over broad public search.
- The Hard Gate remains highly effective when fed real failure cases from named competitors and past hype.
- 5 people is usually the practical maximum before quality degrades.

## How These Learnings Were Captured

- 20 controlled composition experiments (documented in council-experiments.md)
- 9 live real-world uses with diverse seeds (documented in live-runs-9.md)
- Iterative updates to SKILL.md, reference.md, examples.md, and the decision guide after each phase

These findings supersede earlier assumptions about council structure. The skill has moved from a fixed 7-person model to a modular, evidence-based system of small Reality Teams with two official modes.

Update this file whenever new live runs produce consistent, high-signal patterns that should become permanent guidance.

## Security & Refusal Policy (Added During Security Review)

During the formal security review (2026-05-31), the following safeguards were added:

- Explicit **Scope and Refusal Policy** in SKILL.md prohibiting generation of ideas that are clearly illegal or primarily intended to cause harm, defraud, or enable criminal activity.
- Updated **Antagonizer** mandate to explicitly call out legal, ethical, and harm-related issues.
- Added 6th Quick Reject Red Flag in the Hard Gate for "clear intent to cause harm or violate laws".
- These changes were made in response to the identified gap that the previous gates focused on business viability but not on direct harmful intent.

All future updates to the council or Hard Gate must preserve these refusal mechanisms.
